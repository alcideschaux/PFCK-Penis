PFCK in Penile Cancer

Title: Expression Status and Prognostic Value of PDL1, FOXP3, CD8 and Ki67 in Penile Squamous Cell Carcinomas

Authors’ list: Margaret Cocks (1), Diana Taheri (1), Mark W. Ball (2), Stephania M. Bezerra (1), Alan Meeker (1), Maria del Carmen Rodriguez (1), Alcides Chaux (1,4), Arthur Burnett (2), George Netto (1,2,3)

Authors’ affiliations: Departments of (1) Pathology, (2) Urology and (3) Oncology, The Johns Hopkins Medical Institutions, Baltimore, MD; (4) Department of Scientific Research, Norte University, Asuncion, PRY.

Last updated: Tue Aug 9 11:08:29 2016

# Loading the dataset
load("../Data/PFCK_penile.Rdata")

Introduction

In this study we evaluate the immunohistochemical expression of PDL1, FOXP3, CD8, and Ki67 in penile squamous cell carcinomas, assessing the association between expression level and clinicopathologic and outcome features.

The dataset includes data on 53 patients with invasive squamous cell carcinoma. Using representative FFPE tissue samples from the patients’ tumors, 2 high-density tissue microarrays (TMAs) were built at the Johns Hopkins TMA Lab Core (Baltimore, MD; http://tmalab.jhmi.edu/), according to a previously described protocol. Tumors were spotted 3-5 times each using 1.6-mm cores.

Material & method

Evaluation of expression

For each marker, the methodology for evaluation of the immunohistochemical expression was as follows:

  • PDL1: PDL1 was evaluated in tumor and stromal cells, noting the percentage of positive cells.
  • FOXP3, CD8, and Ki67: The expression of these markers was evaluated in lymphocytes infiltrating tumor and stroma, counting the number of positive lymphocytes.

In all cases, the maximum value of all TMA spots was selected to summarize the expression levels of the marker under evaluation. Thus, for each patient, we had a maximum value per marker across all sampled tissues. We then categorized each case as positive or negative for the corresponding marker using either any percentage of positive cells (PDL1) or counts above the mean value of positive cells (FOXP3, CD8, and Ki67).

Additionally, we created a 2-level category of the CD8-to-FOXP3 balance comparing the counts of positive cells in tumor and in stroma, as follows:

  • CD8 counts greater than FOXP3 counts
  • CD8 counts less than or equal to FOXP3 counts

In the following sections, “Tumor lymphocytes” means “lymphocytes infiltrating tumor”. Similarly, “Stromal lymphocytes” means “lymphocytes infiltrating stroma”.


Statistical approach

For each marker, we used the following statistical approach:

  • Association analysis: We compared the following clinicopathologic features per postive/negative levels of the marker:
    • Age (in years) and race
    • Histologic grade
    • Tumor thickness
    • Presence of lymphovascular invasion, perineural invasion, urethral invasion, or lymph node metastasis
    • Tumor invasion of the surgical margin
    • pT stage, pN stage, and tumor stage
    • HPV status (established by in situ hybridization)
    • Development of tumor recurrence, tumor progression, or distant metastasis during follow-up
    • Overall mortality (i.e., all-cause death)
    • Cancer-reletad mortality (i.e., death caused by tumor dissemination)
  • Outcome analysis: We plotted survival curves and estimated odds and hazard ratios considering the expression levels of the marker as the predictor variable and the overall and cancer-related mortality as the outcome variable.

We also used the previous approach considring the CD8-to-FOXP3 balance in tumor lymphocytes and stromal lymphocytes. Finally, we evaluated the correlation between the expression values of all markers.


Statistical tests

For the asssociation analysis we used the Student’s t test/Mann-Whitney U test and the Pearson’s chi-squared test/Fisher’s exact test. For the outcome analysis, we plotted survival curves using the Kaplan-Meier method and we compared them using the log-rank test. Also, we estimated odds ratios and hazard ratios using unconditiona binary logistic regression models and Cox’s proportional hazards regression models. The correlation between markers was evaluated using Pearson’s correlation coefficient. Data was analyzed using R version 3.3.1 (2016-06-21).
